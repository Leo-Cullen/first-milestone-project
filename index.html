About

Who are we?

Established in 2006 in the British Midlands as LMC Solutions LTD, we are a small but thriving company. Our three engineers have thirty years of combined experience.

We pride ourselves on providing the highest levels of quality and service. Always willing to go the extra mile with competitive prices, our excellent references and full insuarance guarantee your peace of mind.

What do we do?

Projects that we have worked on range from individual houses to complex steel frame structures, pedestrian schemes, highways, concrete buildings, roads, car parks, sewage plants, schools and an athletics ground.

How do we do it?

We use Leica robotic survey equipment to collect, process and edit survey information. Together with our CAD expertise we can supply information in AutoCAD and other major formats.

Accuracy is paramount to us. Therefore, our strict quality quality assurance procedures ensure effective checking systems are in place at all stages of the production process.

Bloomfield Road is a football stadium in Blackpool, Lancashire, England, which has been the home of Blackpool F.C. since 1901. The record attendance at Bloomfield Road is 38,098, when Blackpool played Wolverhampton Wanderers on 17 September 1955. The stadium hosted three matches of the 2005 UEFA Women's Championship. Steel Frame Structure

Steel frame is a building technique with steel columns and beams.

The rolled steel "profile" or cross section of steel columns takes the shape of the letter "I". The two wide flanges of a column are thicker and wider than the flanges on a beam, to better withstand compressive stress in the structure. Square and round tubular sections of steel can also be used, often filled with concrete. Steel beams are connected to the columns with bolts and threaded fasteners, and historically connected by rivets. The central "web" of the steel I-beams is often wider than a column web to resist the higher bending moments that occur in beams.
